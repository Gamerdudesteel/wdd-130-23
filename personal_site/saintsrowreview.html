Saint's Row(2006)

Fun, but frustrating

Saint's row one is one of those games that was well recieved at the time at came out but did NOT age well. This game which came out waaay back in 2006. I was only 10 at the time and had never played or even heard of it at the time but decades later, when the half baked reboot flopped in 2022 I kept hearing about how good saints row 2 is. I'm not the kind of person who playes games out of order so I decided to play the entire series and i'm glad I did.

Saint's row came out at a time when it had no compitition. It was obviosly "inspired" by the grand theft auto series but the last console gta came out 2 years prior and the handheld gta liberty city stories simply didn't satisfy gamers need for urban carnage. Enter saints row. Despite this games 80 something percent metacritic score, this game pissed me off. and yet, I couldn't stop playing. The weapons have no precision aiming (except for the sniper rifle) so you have to "hip fire" them. in order to progress the story you need to complete missions (I shouldn't have to explain what these are) and in order to UNLOCK the missions you need to fill a "respect" bar (this bar has no other purpose). This bar is filled by completing multiple "activities" and to be quite honest these activities are quite humerous and entertaining. at first. Activites range from "insurance fraud" where you repeatedly throw yourself into traffic to mayhem where the goal is to cause as much destruction as possible. However with each level of these activities the difficulty increses and the room for errer decreases. these activites go from amusing diversion to frustrating chore in relatively little time .Little room for error is the theme of this entire game. The missions themselves are enjoyable enough. There are three "branches" which can be started and finished in any order (as long as you have enough respect and have completed the previous mission) these missions have no checkpoints, if you die or fail for any other reason then you have to start the entire thing over. sometimes failing a mission isn't even your fault. there had been more than a few occasions where I drove into a pit too small for my car but too big to get out of and i HAD to use THAT car, or when someone else was driving and i'm shooting and the ai gets stuck on a log and gives up (you cant exit the car and finish the mission yourself) and the only option is to restart. I guess that's all the negatives.I mean i DID play through the entire thing so something must be worth it. The game itself has a rich charecter editor, a large fleet of vehicles, a bustling city FILLED with things to do, a decent sense of humor, a large arsenal, you can dress your charecter as santa(amongst other lesser outfits) and i did, the story is engaging and satisfying to complete and the graphics arent terrible for 2006. overall i give this game a 6 out of 10 (for its age)

Saint's row 2

The best of the bunch (by far)and the perfect sequel

saint's row 2 came out in 2008, the same year as it's direct competitor gta 4 (and my favorite pc game, spore) I've played both gta 4 and sr2 and only finished saints row, so what does that tell you? I'm not going to lie here, there had been times playing saints row 1 where i felt overwhelmed and considered using cheats or even skipping the game and just watching the cutscnes online (I didn't and i'm glad) I never once felt that way playing saint's row 2. sr2 added all the features sr1 needed like precision aiming and mission checkpoints and also added features I did't even know I needed like the ability to weild various objects as temporary weapons. and drive new vehicles such as boats, motorcycles, helicopters, and planes. You still need respect to play missions but it doesnt feel like as much of a chore this time around because you can earn respect DURING missions and other ways as well. It's rewarded through kills and stunts and the activities are more fun and forgiving. You can also use "style points" to increase the amount of respect you can earn by eprgading various "cribs" around the map and buying clothes. my personal favoite is one where you pretend to be a cop for a "reality" tv show and commit various acts of police brutality "flamethowers are standard issue right?" saint's row 2 also allows you to choose a voice for your charecter. unlike in saints row 1 where your charecters voice was tied to his race (and only had 5 lines each) saints row 2 allows you to choose from 3 male and 3 female voices. I really can't think of anything wrong with this game. It had a great story and it was fun. Really what else is there to say? 10/10

Saint's row the third

The series takes a left turn into a goofy tone

This game takes 3 steps forward and 2 steps back. They decided to drop the whole "gangster" attitude of the charerects so now they're gangsters who arent, you know.. gangster. This game is more linear. You have to complete all the main missions in order so you don't get to decide what gang goes down first. the healing item menu slots have been replaced with grenade slots, I honestly prefer being able to heal mid battle than use a grenade. The missions don't need respect to play anymore so you can just plow right through the game if you want. and I did. on the plus side, there is now a level up system to upgrade your charecter with things like faster healing and running which makes respect actually useful. you can also upgrade your weapons too. The art style for this game is completly different, the charecters are more muscular. I played the remastered version which crashed about 6 times turing my 13 hour playthrough. overall I think this game took a turn for the worse and as much as i enjoyed it. it wasn't saints row 2. 7/10

Saint's row 4

superman meets the matrix meets any alien movie

My biggest complaint about saints row 4 is that it reuses the same map as saints row 3. But at the same time it doesnt really matter that much because most of the missions take place outside of the world map. My second complaint is that the super speed that your charecter unlocks quite early in the game makes every vehicle obsolete. my third complaint about this games is the tone and atmosphere. If you thought saints row the third was all over the place then saints row 4 throws any noralicy straight out of the window. the grenade slot has been replaced yet again with a power slot. The 4 powers in these slots, blast, stomp, buff, and telekenisis are very powerful and interesting but take way too long to recharge to be "super" usefull so unlike cars you will still need to keep your weapons around, full, and upgraded because your combat powers don't cut it by themselves. The story in this game is it's worst element. It completely ignores the gangster element of the first three games and replaces it with this bizzare superhero matrix thing where you have to stop aliens. After playing through it to the end i'd have to say that the strongest part of this game are the missions. They're admitidly more enjoyable than the ones in it's predicesor but that alone doesnt save this game. 6/10

Saint's row: Gat out of hell

Ambitious Dlc turned standlone game

Gat out of hell takes place an indeterminate amount of time after saints row 4. The player charecter from the previous 4 games (Or previous 2 games if you subscribe to the theory the main charecter was somehow replaced between 2 and 3) Is sucked into hell and Johnny Gat (and kinzie but be real, this games about johnny) Must go rescue them. That's all about the story i'm gonna touch. We went from a modern gangster story to dante's inferno (if that's nnot at least similar to the plot of dante's inferno thats cause i never read the book, all i know is the main charecter goes to hell. (or at least thats what i gather)) Gat out of hell is enjoyable with it's new flight mechanic (yes the superhero themed saints row 4 was completely lacking flight from its roster of powers.) And it's new setting of "new hades" the "capital of hell" is interesting with several distrects. Now for the critisism. The whole game is only 4 an a half hours long. For a 15 dollar game that's not fantastic but i've heard worse. Secondly, this game has ZERO customization. It's expected not to have a charecter creator since you play as Fan favorite musclehead gun nut Johnny gat and thats fine, but there are no clothing stores, custom car garages, or weapon skins. You do not get to make this game your own in any way. Problem number three and by far the biggest one is there are only 3 traditional missions, the rest of the missions involve completing simmilar tasks and activities from previous games. Problem number 4 (im gonna combine 2 cause they go hand in hand) Is the vehivle variety and lack of music. There are only like 4 different cars in the game and there are no radio stations or music of any kind. previous saint's row games had excelent soundtracks. This one had no soundtrack.This game isn't all negative, the weapons are fun, the powers are useful. The dialog is enntertaining, its fun, don't get me wrong. It's just not enough to justify spending 15 dollars on. 5/10
